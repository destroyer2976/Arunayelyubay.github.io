Boeing 777 Full-Scale SolidWorks Modeling

This project involves the high-precision 3D modeling of a Boeing 777 aircraft. The focus was on mastering complex assembly management and aerodynamic surface modeling to create a functional digital twin of the aircraft.

Highlights

  • Blueprint-Based Precision: Developed the aircraft model based on authentic aviation blueprints, ensuring accurate proportions for the fuselage, wingspan, and tail section.
  • Advanced Surfacing Techniques: Utilized Lofted and Boundary Surface tools to create complex aerodynamic geometries and seamless transitions between the wings and fuselage (Wing-Body Fairing).
  • High-Detail Component Modeling: Engineered critical aircraft assemblies, including engine nacelles, intake systems, and detailed vertical and horizontal stabilizers.
  • Parametric Design Workflow: Applied a parametric modeling approach, allowing for efficient design iterations while maintaining full assembly integrity.
  • Material & Visual Analysis: Configured realistic material properties to simulate aluminum skin and composite structures, enhancing the model's visual and technical fidelity.

Tools & Technologies

Software: SolidWorks (Advanced Surfacing, Assembly Design, Motion Study).

Core Techniques: Blueprint-based Modeling, Lofted & Swept Surfaces, Boundary Surface, Spline Manipulation.

Mechanical Analysis: Complex Assembly Mating, Interference Detection, Exploded View Animation.

Visualization: PhotoView 360 / SolidWorks Visualize for industrial-grade rendering.
